The Nightwing Approach

Dick Grayson isn't a powerlifter — he's an acrobat. His physique is built on bodyweight control, explosive movement, and rotational strength. For you that means: compound lifts to build a base, calisthenics to develop body mastery, and cardio that's low-impact enough to stay safe with asthma. Consistency over intensity, always. You're building a foundation here — 3 months from now this routine will feel easy, which is exactly the goal.
⚠️

Asthma — Keep Your Inhaler Accessible

Always have a rescue inhaler nearby. Warm up for at least 5–10 min before any cardio. Prefer indoor training on high-pollen or cold days. Stop if you feel tightness before wheezing starts — it's easier to prevent an attack than stop one.

🩸

Prediabetes — Exercise Is Your Best Medication

Strength training and cardio both improve insulin sensitivity. Never work out on an empty stomach — a small complex-carb snack 30–45 min before training stabilizes blood sugar. Stay hydrated throughout. Working out 3× a week is genuinely powerful for reversing prediabetes.

Prediabetes Strategy

Blood Sugar First

Prioritize low glycemic index carbs. Eat protein + fat before carbs at every meal to blunt glucose spikes. Never eat carbs alone.

Lactose-Free Protein

Skip the Dairy

No whey protein needed. Eggs, chicken, fish, lean beef, canned tuna, tempeh, and edamame are your best friends. Oat or pea protein powder if you want a shake.

Asthma + Diet

Anti-Inflammatory Focus

Omega-3s (salmon, sardines, walnuts) help reduce airway inflammation. Vitamin D is consistently linked to better asthma control — get sunlight or supplement.

Pre-Workout Meal

30–45 Min Before

Small meal or snack: oats + eggs, rice cakes + peanut butter, or banana + a boiled egg. Stabilizes blood sugar for the session.

Hydration is non-negotiable

Aim for at least 80–100 oz of water daily. Dehydration worsens both asthma symptoms and blood sugar regulation. Keep a bottle with you during every workout.
